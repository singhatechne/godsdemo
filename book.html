Lung Cancer Treatment

Advanced diagnostics and personalized treatment plans for lung cancer patients

About Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the lungs. Your lungs are two spongy organs in your chest that take in oxygen when you inhale and release carbon dioxide when you exhale.

People who smoke have the greatest risk of lung cancer, though lung cancer can also occur in people who have never smoked.

Common Symptoms

  • A new cough that doesn't go away
  • Coughing up blood, even a small amount
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest pain
  • Hoarseness
  • Losing weight without trying
  • Bone pain
  • Headache

Treatment Options

  • Surgery
  • Radiation therapy
  • Chemotherapy
  • Targeted drug therapy

Advanced Treatment Options

Targeted Therapy

Targeted therapy uses drugs to target specific genes and proteins that are involved in the growth and survival of cancer cells. This treatment can block the growth and spread of cancer cells while limiting damage to healthy cells.

Immunotherapy

Immunotherapy helps your immune system fight cancer. It uses substances made by the body or in a laboratory to improve or restore immune system function.

Diagnostic Procedures

Imaging Tests

Imaging tests allow your doctor to look for a mass or lesion in your lungs. These may include X-rays, CT scans, MRI scans, and PET scans to detect abnormalities and determine the extent of cancer spread.

Biopsy Procedures

A biopsy is the only way to definitively diagnose lung cancer. During a biopsy, your doctor removes a small sample of cells from the suspicious area for laboratory testing.

Rehabilitation

Pulmonary rehabilitation can help you regain your strength and improve your breathing after lung cancer treatment. This program includes exercise training, nutrition advice, and education about your condition.

Prevention & Screening

Risk Reduction

The most important thing you can do to prevent lung cancer is to not smoke and to avoid secondhand smoke. Other preventive measures include testing your home for radon and avoiding carcinogens at work.

Screening Programs

Annual lung cancer screening with low-dose CT scans is recommended for people at high risk of lung cancer due to smoking history. Early detection significantly improves treatment outcomes.

Blood Cancer Treatment

Comprehensive care for leukemia, lymphoma, and myeloma

About Blood Cancer

Blood cancer is a type of cancer that affects your blood cells. Most blood cancers start in the bone marrow where blood is produced.

There are three main types of blood cancer: leukemia, lymphoma, and myeloma. Each type affects different cells and has different treatment approaches.

Common Symptoms

  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fever or chills
  • Persistent fatigue and weakness
  • Frequent infections
  • Easy bruising or bleeding
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Bone pain or tenderness
  • Night sweats

Treatment Options

  • Chemotherapy
  • Radiation therapy
  • Immunotherapy
  • Targeted therapy
  • Stem cell transplant
  • CAR T-cell therapy
